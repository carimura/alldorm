Detailed Information:

Lamp Care & Operation
Do's & Don'ts:
DO have the motion lamp at room temperature before starting.
DO allow the lamp to warm up for four to six hours to insure it performs at
its optimal level.
DO operate lamp on flat and stationary surfaces which are at room
temperature.
DO keep lamp and cord out of reach of small children's and pets.
DO use only a 40 watt, 120 volt, type A15 frosted appliance/ceiling fan bulb
to replace a burned out light bulb.
DO handle the glass globe with extreme care. The globe may break if dropped,
knocked over or subject to any form of sudden impact. If it becomes
necessary to clean up broken glass, then use care and sufficient safety
precautions. (Refer to Clean Up Instructions)
DO check to see if the light bulb and socket in the base are completely
centered and tightened. The light bulb and socket may have moved during
shipping. If this has occurred, then gently use your fingers to re-center
it. Before touching the lamp, make sure it is cool and unplugged.
DO exercise caution when handling the lamp when it is plugged in. It takes 2
or 3 hours for the lamp to cool once it is turned off.
DO realize that if your particular lamp has a ceramic base, then the base
will retain heat. If the lamp is running too hot, then lower the setting on
the adjustable dimmer switch.

DON'T place the lamp near sources of heat or cold, such as a heater or air
conditioner. The lamp operates best at temperatures between 21¯C/68¯F to
23¯C/72¯F.
DON'T move or shake the lamp while it is HOT; doing so may cause it to
permanently malfunction.
DON'T operate or store the lamp in direct sunlight; doing so may cause the
colors to fade.
DON'T try to force the polarized plug into an outlet. As a safety
precaution, one blade on the plug is wider than the other and can only be
inserted one way into an outlet.
DON'T remove the CAP on the globe or add any fluids or solids to the lamp:
ALL WARRANTIES ARE VOID IF THE GLOBE HAS BEEN OPENED.
DON'T operate the lamp on Glass Surfaces.
DON'T operate the lamp for more than 10 Hours at a time.
